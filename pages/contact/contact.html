How does the ideal weight calculator work?

The weight calculator determines a person's ideal weight based on their height and gender. The calculator computes the height (in feet & inches) based on the following formulas:
For men:

D. R. Miller’s formula (1983): 56.2 kg + 1.41 kg per inch over 5 feet

J.D. Robinson’s formula (1983): 52 kg + 1.9 kg per inch over 5 feet

B. J. Devine’s formula (1974): 50.0 kg + 2.3 kg per inch over 5 feet

G. J. Hamwi’s formula (1964): 48.0 kg + 2.7 kg per inch over 5 feet

For women:

D. R. Miller’s formula (1983): 53.1 kg + 1.36 kg per inch over 5 feet

J.D. Robinson’s formula (1983): 49 kg + 1.7 kg per inch over 5 feet

B. J. Devine’s formula (1974): 45.5 kg + 2.3 kg per inch over 5 feet

G. J. Hamwi’s formula (1964): 45.5 kg + 2.2 kg per inch over 5 feet



How do I use the ideal weight calculator?

To use the calculator, your height must be 5 feet and above. Simply input your height in feet & inches and your gender. The application will then present your ideal weight according to Miller (1983), Robinson (1983), Devine (1974), and Hamwi (1964). If your height is below the required value, the calculator will display and "error" output.
